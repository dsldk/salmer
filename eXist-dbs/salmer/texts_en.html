Texts

Hymn books

  • Ludwig Dietz, A new Handbook with Hymns and Spiritual Praises (1529) The oldest extant Danish hymn book comprising 72 hymns in Danish besides instructions for the divine service; without music notation
  • Christiern Pedersen, The Malmö Hymn book (1533) Hymn book published by the famous Danish Renaissance writer Christiern Pedersen comprising 125 hymns in Danish besides instructions for the divine service; without music notation
  • Ludwig Dietz, Some new Hymns and Praises (1536) Supplement to Dietz’ hymn book from 1529 comprising 35 hymns in Danish; without music notation
  • Hans Vingaard, A new hymn book (1553) Hymn book comprising 163 hymns in Danish, 6 of which are accompanied by music notation, besides 18 hymns in Latin, and instructions for the divine service
  • Hans Thomissøn, The Danish hymn book (1569) The first royally authorized hymn book of Denmark and Norway comprising 268 hymns mostly in Danish, 216 of which are accompanied by music notation, besides instructions for the divine service, and detailed information about the hymns

Service books

  • Claus Mortensen Tøndebinder, The Christian Service (1529) The oldest extant Lutheran service book in Danish comprising the permanent hymns of the service accompanied by empty printed staves and written notes in two
  • Oluf Ulricksøn, A handbook comprising the holy Evangelical Service (1535) Service book in Danish comprising printed music notation to the Lord's Prayer and the Words of Institution, instructions for baptism, wedding, sick call, and funeral
  • Frands Vormordsen, Handbook of the true Evangelical Service (1539) Service book in Danish comprising printed music notation to the sung liturgy parts besides instructions for baptism, wedding, sick call, funeral, and finally prayers for the ecclesiastical year
  • Niels Jespersen, Gradual. A common songbook (1573) Royally authorized songbook comprising melodies and texts for the sundays and holidays of the ecclesiastical year and some other services. 140 texts are in Latin, and 65 in Danish

The Bible

  • The Danish Bible of Christian III The first Danish translation of all parts of the Bible: the Old Testament, the Apocrypha, and the New Testament
